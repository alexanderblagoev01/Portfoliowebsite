02  — The Brief
A venue that opens only for special occasions.

Restaurant Bogota is not your typical dining establishment. It's a private events venue — a space that opens its doors only when there's a reason to celebrate. Weddings, anniversaries, milestone birthdays, corporate gatherings. The kind of evenings people remember for years.

The challenge was building a brand that matched that sense of occasion — dark, warm, and quietly luxurious — without feeling cold or corporate. Every element had to feel like it belonged at the table.

The result is a complete visual system built around deep black, Bogota orange, and a typographic pairing that balances authority with warmth. Designed from scratch — logo, website, and physical menu — all produced and delivered as a cohesive whole.

04  — Physical Menu Print Design
Designed to be held.

The physical menu was designed as an extension of the brand — not a separate deliverable, but a continuation of the same visual language. Same palette, same typography, same feeling as the website and the room itself.

Every page was treated as a layout problem: how do you present a curated menu in a way that feels considered, unhurried, and worthy of the occasion? The answer was restraint — generous white space, orange accents, and type that lets the food speak.
